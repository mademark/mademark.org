# License

Made Mark uses a split licensing model — the same approach Creative Commons
uses for its own work. The openness is in the specification. The protection
is in the signal.

## The specification — CC0 1.0 Universal (Public Domain)

The Made Mark vocabulary, label syntax (plain-language and short forms), URI
structure, and JSON-LD context (`ns/index.json` and everything under
`marks/*/index.json`) are dedicated to the public domain under
[CC0 1.0](https://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/zero/1.0/).

You may implement, adapt, and build on the specification without asking
permission and without attribution, though attribution is appreciated.

## The mark icons — CC BY-ND 4.0

and `assets/png/` depicting the Human Made, Human Designed AI Made, and AI
Made icons) are licensed under
[CC BY-ND 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nd/4.0/).

You are free to use these icons **unmodified** to identify Made Mark
authorship on content. You may not modify, recolor, restyle, or otherwise
alter them, or use them outside the context of Made Mark attribution.

Credit: Made Mark, mademark.org.

## Site source code

The HTML, CSS, and JavaScript that make up mademark.org itself (this
repository, excluding the specification files and icons covered above) are
provided as reference implementation. Reuse of the tooling code is welcome
in the spirit of the open specification; the Made Mark name/logo
restrictions above still apply to any resulting product or fork.
